\chapter{Methodology}
\label{cha:methodology}

Depending on the nature of the thesis, this chapter title can also be \glqq Concept Design\grqq.

Description of the initial situation and the subject environment. If applicable, the relevant boundary conditions and influential factors to be taken into account are discussed.

Requirements analysis and definition of requirements, structured where possible, in order to traceably verify the requirements at a later stage.

Derivation of a solution (a methodology, an experimental setup, or various concepts), evaluation of solutions, and conscious selection of the chosen approach. At this point, the reliability of a methodology or the accuracy of investigations should also be addressed. These considerations are intended to help meet the defined requirements with the proposed solution in order to ultimately achieve the goals of the thesis.

When comparing different solution approaches, a utility value analysis (weighted decision matrix) can be helpful, for example. In doing so, not only aspects such as functionality, performance, feasibility, and usability should be considered, but also economic aspects such as unit costs, development costs, or resource consumption. Aspects of sustainability considering the entire product life cycle of a developed solution are also highly significant.

Both in requirement definition and in finding solutions, there is a wide range of different methods. A small selection can be found in the following list:

\begin{itemize}
	\item Requirement definition using Requirements Engineering \autocite{Pohl.2021}
	\item Systems Engineering approach \autocite{Schluter.2023}
	\item Agile development methodologies \autocite{Cohn.2010, Martin.2020, Wirdemann.2022}
	\item Classical evaluation techniques \autocite{Breiing.1997, Zangemeister.2014}
\end{itemize}

The objective of this chapter is to develop a variety of solutions based on comprehensively and precisely formulated requirements (including non-goals, if applicable), which are subsequently evaluated in a structured manner to derive a sound justification for the intended type of implementation.
