\chapter{Introduction}
\label{cha:introduction}

The following key points can be used to structure the introduction:

\begin{itemize}
	\item Introduction, rationale, purpose, and objective of the task
	\item Explanation of the problem statement
	\item Specification of the task to be solved
	\item Formulation of a core research question, if applicable
	\item Initial situation, planned approach, methodology, and target outcome
	\item At the end of the introduction, a brief overview of the chapter structure is provided: \glqq The thesis is structured as follows: \ldots\grqq
\end{itemize}

The introduction is usually written as a continuous text spanning one to two pages. A further subdivision into numbered sections is not recommended, as this is uncommon, does not enhance readability, and complicates the formulation of the introduction. Further recommendations regarding the structure of the introduction and the overall document can be found, for example, in \autocite{DHBW.2021} and \autocite{Lindenlauf.2022}.
